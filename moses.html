Today's world is full of pressure. It is easy to feel small. Let's see how Moses followed God.

Self-Doubt

Giants

Step Out

Obeying in Small Steps

Moses had many fears. He told God he could not speak well. But God did not need him to be perfect. He only wanted Moses to obey. Today, do not be afraid of bosses or managers. Speak with courage because God is with you.

What Moses Teaches Us

Four wonders that show God's power in our weakness.

The Burning Bush (Exodus 3:2)

God called a humble shepherd from a fire.

The Ten Plagues (Exodus 7-12)

God showed His power over false idols.

Parting the Red Sea (Exodus 14:21)

God opened a path of dry land through the sea.

Manna & Water (Exodus 16-17)

God provided food and water in the dry desert.

Remember Who You Serve

God is real and powerful. He does amazing things. When you talk to bosses or managers at work, remember who you serve. You serve the King of kings. You do not need to be afraid of anyone.

Moses' Journey

Read the story of Moses, the man who led God's people out of Egypt.

HEBREWS 11:27 PROGRESSION

1
Step 01 - Exodus 3:4

God called Moses from a burning bush.

God told Moses His plan to rescue the Israelites.

2
Step 02 - Exodus 5:1

Moses stood before Pharaoh.

Moses obeyed God and demanded freedom for the people.

3
Step 03 - Exodus 7:14

God sent ten plagues.

God showed His power to both Egypt and His people.

4
Step 04 - Exodus 12:21

The Passover sacrifice.

God protected the households marked with the blood of the lamb.

5
Step 05 - Exodus 14:21

God split the Red Sea.

God made a dry path through the sea to escape Pharaoh's army.

6
Step 06 - Exodus 20:1

God gave the Commandments.

God wrote His laws on stone tablets on Mount Sinai.

7
Step 07 - Exodus 25:8

Building the Tabernacle.

The people built a sacred tent for God's presence to dwell.

8
Step 08 - Deuteronomy 34:1

Moses trusted God's promise.

Moses looked at the Promised Land, trusting God to lead the people in.
